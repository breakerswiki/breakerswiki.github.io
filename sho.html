Sho Kamui

Backstory

Sho Kamui is a master of Karate and the protagonist of Breakers Revenge. He enters the FIST Tournament in order to prove the strength of his martial art and to face off against his old rival, Lee Dao-Long, who gave him the scar on his cheek.

Overview

Sho is a well-rounded character with a wide range of offensive options, from powerful specials to a safe air fireball super and a slick divekick that works from any jump.

He's not your typical zoner despite his strong fireballs; Instead, he's all about keeping the pressure on with his jumps/divekick and dashes. Once he's got you in the corner, it's tough to escape.

And it's not just about offense either – Sho has strong defensive moves like his reversal (214K) and a effective crouch High Punch.

No matter where he is on the screen, Sho remains a threat.

Basic knowledge

Anti-air

Go with either 214HK or cr.HP > 214HK.
In most matchups, cr.HP is the more reliable choice.

Break combos

Use 214HK as your breaker move due to its invincibility Learn how to counter breakable combos in the Matchups section.

Learn how to switch sides

End combos with 236HK or 236LK to switch sides. It's useful for escaping corners.
example: cr.LK, cr.LP > cr.HP > 236HK.

Take advantage of the aerial game

j.LK
Super-cancelable, and effective against air-to-air normals and some special moves.

Divekick

Using divekicks in pressure is a good way to try to open your opponent's defense.

strong options with divekick:

  • Counter a jump attempt or a button press
  • Fuzzy with j.HK > Super
  • Cancel the divekick startup into a super
  • mess up your opponent's anti-air timing
⚠️On hit, divekicks are breakable (throw, reversal, backdash)

Be careful with far st.HP

⚠️ Don't overuse it in combos, it's breakable in many situations. If you're close enough, go for cr.HP or finish your combo with cr.HK.

You should use these combo routes:
- far: cr.LK, cr.LK > cr.HK > Super
- close: cr.LK, cr.LP > cr.HP > LK+HP > Super

Chip damage

When the opponent has very little health, you can try to finish them off with 464P or 236HK.
464P does the most chip damage. You can try to use it just after a divekick.

Fireball pressure and okizeme

When your opponent's down, 236P > Run is a solid okizeme. then, you can do a run-stop and react, or just cancel the run with an anti-air move.

Fuzzy

Trigger your opponent's guard with a jump attack that forces them to block high, then before they recover from blockstun, either go low or high again

Fuzzy examples:

  • j.LK > j.HK
  • j.HK > j.HK
  • j.HK > j.HK > Super
  • divekick > j.HK > Super

    Onigawara +

    Confirm the overhead on hit with 646P, Super, or 236K.
    Performing overhead > fireball on corner leaves you at a disadvantage, even on hit or block. You can also get punished by an invincible super during the fireball startup.

    Tobihizageri +

    This move can become risky if opponents are familiar with the matchup. Otherwise, you may either be in throw range or leave a gap during the knee animation that the opponent can punish

    cr.LK, cr.LK > cr.HP > LK+HP
    you're in the throw range but there is no gap.
    cr.LK, cr.LP, cr.LK > cr.HP > LK+HP
