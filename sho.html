Sho Kamui

History

Sho Kamui, Breakers Revenge's Karate master and protagonist, joins the FIST Tournament to showcase his martial art's power and confront his old rival, Lee Dao-Long, responsible for the scar on his cheek.

Gameplay

Sho is a versatile character with plenty of strong cancelable moves and a divekick that works from any jump angle. His air attacks and dashes help to keep constant pressure, making it hard to escape when cornered.

Defense

Anti-air
Use 214K, 5C (close) or 2C for anti-air. If Sho’s 2C is beaten by any aerial moves, use 214K for its startup invincibility.

Almost all of Sho's normals have juggle properties and are special and super-cancelable, allowing more damage with conversions into 214K or any super.

Breaker
Break with throw, 214K or backdash.

Jump B
jB is one of Sho's best moves. it’s super-cancelable on whiff or hit, comes fast, remains active, and has good priority over normals. In some situations, you can use it as a wake-up option.

Offense

Combos
Sho has numerous combo possibilities thanks to the game's juggle properties and his mostly cancelable normals. Check the combos section for more info.

⚠️ Avoid using 5C in grounded combos, as it often leads to breakable situations. If you're close, finish with 2C > 214K or 2D > 236236P instead.

Jump attacks
As mentioned earlier, jB is an excellent choice. On whiff, you can also input an air super that is safe on block, similar to jA.

A close-to-the-ground air-to-air jD enables re-jump combos due to its juggling properties.

When you have meter, jumps enhance your aerial game and make opponents hesitant to anti-air. Use low-height dive kicks and jump attacks for close-range pressure. With meter, you can create a strong high/low mix-up using fuzzy jump or 2B.

⚠️ Jumps and divekicks can be vulnerable to the break mechanic, including throws, invincible reversals, and backdashes.

Movement
To close distance and find safer openings, cancel Sho's dash with 2B or 5C; both recover quickly and are safe on block.

Super usage

Seiken Shou Ha


It's great for punishing unsafe moves and serves as a good combo ender after 2D

Geki Rekkou Ha


The move is safe on block, useful to bait anti-air, cancelable from any air normal, and can be used after a whiffed jA or jB

Kurenai Gansai Shuu


It can hit from long range, has an invincible startup that allows it to go through projectiles, and serves as a breaker.

Knee

Knee can be risky depending on range. You might end up in throw range or leave a punishable gap in the knee animation. However, when the knee hits on block, it seems even, allowing you to counter a throw attempt with 214K.

Okizeme

When your opponent's on the ground, using a fireball into Run is a solid okizeme. You can then either do a run-stop and react, or simply cancel the run with an anti-air move.

Chip damage

To finish off your low-health opponent, use 464P or 236D.
464P deals the most chip damage. You can try using it immediately after a divekick.

Fuzzy

Force a high block with a jump attack, then mix up with low or high before their recovery.

Sho excels with his super-cancelable jD into air Super, which is safe and deals significant damage.

Corner reset

Corner reset
Side switch with either 2C > B+C or close 5C.C > B+C, then :
  • walk under (condor)
  • dash empty (sho, rila, tia, saizo, baihu)
  • crouch dash attack (pielle, alsion, maherl, dao-long)

Saizo

  • Saizo's JC is hard to deal with, as it can beat your anti-air normals. Use jB to counter it and to stop wake-up pressure.
  • Use jB and dive kick to counter his zoning, or bait his anti-airs by following up with air Super 236236P.
  • Beware of his "hair" special; it acts as an anti-air with invincibility frames and must be super canceled, as it is unsafe otherwise.

Condor

  • Zone him from long range with 236A, then use short dashes to find openings. you can punish his slides and tomahawks on block or whiff.
  • After landing a dive kick or jump attack, bait his break reversal (360 grab or Supers) by jumping back. If both have meter, be ready to counter with your air super if he uses his.
  • On your wakeup, backroll or backdash if he’s not close to avoid him gaining a threatening position.

Sho

  • A match with frequent short dashes and jumps/divekicks due to Sho's air advantages.
  • Break jumps/divekick hitting the upper body with throw or 214K. Howerver, he can counter your break attempt, as divekick > 214K combos and counters throws.
  • 236K can bait your punish attempt when followed by his air Super, but it’s a risky gimmick he shouldn't use if you have meter.
Ranki Ryuu Geri +

Ranki Ryuu Geri is tricky to punish as it can be followed by air super. However, it is minus on block. If you have meter, a forward jD > air Super guarantees a punish.

Onigawara +

An overhead, safe on block, and cancelable. it's punishable during its startup frames. It's also breakable when canceled into 236K.

Tobihizageri +

Sashikomi Kick +
