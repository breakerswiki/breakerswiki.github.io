Overview

Pielle demands solid fundamentals, opponent reads, and risk-taking. He's challenging due to his slow recovery and lack of invincible reversals without supers.

Neutral

Pielle struggles with zoning. His fireballs have high recovery and are unsafe on block which makes his pressure (e.g., 2Ax2 > 236A) unreliable.

His best options are at close range, where he can poke with 5A or 2A, both of which can be converted into 214P, overhead B+D, or super (236236P).

Anti-air

Anti-air options

  • Thunder Super (214214P)
  • close 5C (effective to counter crossups)
  • slide (2D) useful in against backjumps on landing
  • 214214K can punish jumps from long range

His anti-air special, 623P, has low priority and can leave you vulnerable even on hit. Instead, use his super (214214P) with invulnerable startup, or rely on normals like close jC, 5C, or far 5D.

⚠️ If 623A hits a grounded opponent, you get long recovery and no knockdown, so stick to using it as an anti-air.

Using his slide as an anti-air allows for OTG setups into Super 236236P or 214214P (corner).

Super usage

Robin Super (236236P) goes through projectiles, in some matchups you can punish your opponent projectiles from far away

His thunder super (214214P) is an effective anti-air and his rose super (214214K) can whiff punish or anti-air from max range

Flying Peach Bomber

You can cancel most normals into an overhead. On hit, confirm with A or 5B, and if the opponent is standing, follow up with Rose Train.

⚠️ Be careful when using it ! Cancelling any normal into an overhead does not combo, instead, it creates a gap and leaves you vulnerable. Overhead confirms are also breakable.
