Rila Estansia

History

Rila Estansia is a free-spirited Brazilian from the Amazon. A nature protector, she joins the FIST Tournament to uncover the evil spirit threatening her people.

Rila's alt, Virgo Sandra, was orphaned and raised by a giant snake in the "forest tribe".

Gameplay

Rila is a charge character with high damage output. Even though she’s slow and lacks zoning tools, her strong anti-air and big damage make her really good in close and mid-range fights

Neutral

2C

This move is really frustrating for opponents. It’s hard to punish and safe on block, making it great for shutting down approaches. Use it smartly, as it has few recovery frames, works best from a distance, and is effective as anti-air.

Neutral jC

This can beat most normals and some anti-air specials when they're out of their invincibility frames.

Quick scratch +

Its speed can easily catch your opponent off guard, making it an unexpected and effective move

⚠️ Safe, except against Saizo and Tia.

Quick scratch +

It can be used as an anti-air and combo into super Bloody Fang, dealing much more damage than the LP version but is always unsafe.

Killer Snake +

Can be followed by Bloody Fang Super and is used to punish projectiles at mid and close range

⚠️ On block, you leave few frames for the player to punish, but you can still input super to catch mistimed counters. It's risky but effective against opponents unfamiliar with the matchup.

Anti-air

Wild Saver +

Quick anti-air move; the Heavy version has longer recovery. This move launches diagonally upward but can be ineffective if used too early at close range, as cross-up jumps may make it whiff. In that case, use 5D.

Close 5C

Close range anti-air.

Close 5D

Close 5D covers more range than close 5C. Use it at close range to counter ambiguous jump-in/cross-ups

5A

Convert 5A using Quick Charge C, and then you can follow up with the Bloody Fang Super if you have meter.

Super usage

Bloody Fang +

Use it to punish projectiles and extend combos.

Shine beast +

has few practical applications, but it can still be used as a reversal due to its invincible startup.

Chip damage

Spinning Claw deals the most chip damage, but it's difficult to land safely. Opt for Killer Snake D or 2A > Quick Scratch C instead.

Overhead, negative on hit and block with a long recovery. Leaves ample time for punishment. Saizo rarely resorts to this move unless finishing off his opponent

A slide that must be canceled to be safe. Slide into bomb is safe midscreen against Rila (2B+HK > Hiryuu HP). At close range in the corner, punish slide > bomb with 5A.

Yashagami HP knocks down opponents, while Yashagami LP serves as a reversal and anti-air. This move must be canceled into a super due to its negative frames on hit and block. Be cautious on block; Saizo still has a few frames to execute a super, causing hesitation in your usual punishment.

Tia

Normals
All her normals, even cr.HK, are safe on block, boosting her offense. Tia pressures Sho well with her dash attack, especially dash > st.HK, doubling as a powerful anti-air and setup for a super juggle. To stop her dash attacks, Sho needs to quickly and preemptively input multiple 2B.

Soul Spike

Ground Soul Spike LK/HK are not overhead, block low.
Soul Spike LK has pushback and minimal recovery. Rila can punish with 2B > [4]6C. Soul Spike HK has no pushback, making it easier to punish. Punish with 5C or a Throw.
On hit, you can break both versions with a throw, backdash, or Super.
Before attempting a block punish, check her super meter first. Tia can counter with Burning Revolver. A throw is a guaranteed punish if she uses Soul Spike HK.
